# Operating System & Playbook
### The day-to-day: tool stack, SOPs, automation vs outsourcing, and a phone-run KPI dashboard
**Prepared: 2026-06-26 · Design-led desk/workspace gear brand · Toronto founder · phone-run, outsourced fulfillment**

> Every cross-border tax/legal/customs item below is flagged 🚩 and **must be confirmed with a cross-border CPA, a licensed US customs broker, and (for incorporation) a lawyer.** The numbers here are planning ranges, not filing advice. Sources are cited inline and in Report 04 (Operating Model).

---

## 0. Design principle: the business must fit in a phone

Every system below is chosen because it has a usable mobile app or mobile web dashboard, and because it pushes physical work to a third party. The founder's job is **product, content, and decisions** — not packing boxes or answering every email. The rule of thumb: *if a task is physical, repetitive, or after-hours, it is outsourced or automated.*

## 2. What is automated vs outsourced vs founder-only

| Lane | Tasks | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| **Automated** | Order routing → 3PL/WFS; shipping labels & tracking emails; review-request emails; Klaviyo welcome/abandoned-cart/post-purchase flows; sales-tax calc; content scheduling (Buffer queue); Walmart tax collection/remittance in ~40 states ([Walmart](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Taxes%20&%20payments/Tax%20information/Sales-tax-collection)). |
| **Outsourced** | Pick/pack/ship & returns (3PL + WFS); customs clearance (broker 🚩); bookkeeping/tax filing (CPA 🚩); paid-ad management once scaling (freelance media buyer, later); overflow customer support (VA, ~$5–8/hr, once volume warrants); paid UGC video (creators, $100–300/video). |
| **Founder-only (the real job)** | Product/design decisions & supplier selection; brand & content direction (shoot the hero clips); pricing; which SKUs to add and when; cash decisions; reading the weekly KPI dashboard and acting. |

The founder should personally do **content and product** for as long as possible — those are the moat and cannot be outsourced early. Everything physical or clerical leaves the founder's plate immediately.

## 3. Core SOPs (write these once, follow every time)

### SOP-1 — New product launch (per SKU)
1. Sample → pass the physical acceptance gate (Report 02 §5). 2. Negotiate PO (500–1,000 units) + get HTS classification from broker 🚩. 3. Shoot listing + content assets *from the sample* before stock lands. 4. Build Shopify + Walmart listings (title, 6–8 images, A+ copy, cable-diameter range, magnet hold-force). 5. Inbound to 3PL/WFS. 6. Seed to creators (SOP-4) the week stock lands. 7. Turn on listing; gather first 10–15 reviews before any paid spend.

### SOP-2 — Reorder / inventory (avoid the FBA over-order mistake)
- **Trigger:** reorder when on-hand + in-transit < ~6–8 weeks of trailing 4-week sell-through.
- **First-timer rule:** never reorder more than ~1.5× last cycle's actual sell-through. Cash preservation > stockout avoidance early. A short stockout is survivable; dead inventory killed the last business.
- Track **weeks-of-cover** per SKU per channel on the dashboard.

### SOP-3 — Order & returns exception handling
- Orders flow automatically. Founder only touches **exceptions** (address fails, damaged-in-transit, escalations). 3PL/WFS handle standard returns to a **US returns address**; keep returns inside the US (cross-border returns re-incur duty — Report 04). For low-value items, default policy: **"keep it / partial refund"** rather than pay return freight.

### SOP-4 — Creator seeding (the growth engine)
1. Build a list of 40 micro-creators (5k–50k followers) in desk/home-office/aesthetic niches. 2. DM a short personal note + offer free kit. 3. Ship gifts (cost = COGS + shipping, ~$15–30 each). 4. Expect ~30–50% to post. 5. Repost everything; track which clips/creators drive traffic. 6. Pay the top 3–5 performers for dedicated UGC with **usage rights** ($100–300/video) → reuse as paid creative later. (Benchmarks: [influencer-hero](https://www.influencer-hero.com/blogs/ugc-influencer-rates).)

### SOP-5 — Content production (weekly)
- Batch-shoot 5–7 phone clips/week against the content pillars (satisfying cable-routing, before/after, desk-tour, problem→solution, UGC reposts, behind-the-brand). Queue in Buffer. Daily TikTok + Reels, 3–4 YT Shorts/week, 3–5 Pinterest pins/week. Never raw-link in Reddit (instant ban) — participate 10:1.

### SOP-6 — Weekly close (every Monday, 30–45 min, on the phone)
Pull the dashboard (§5), update weeks-of-cover, check ad ROAS, reconcile cash, decide: reorder? add spend? cut a content format? Anything red → act this week.

## 4. Cross-border operating spine (the part that's easy to get wrong)

This is the structural backbone, condensed from Report 04. **De-minimis is dead (US ended the $800 duty-free threshold Aug 29 2025; ad valorem method since Feb 28 2026)** ([Buckland](https://www.buckland.com/news/de-minimis-exemption-eliminated-for-certain-imports-effective-august-29-2025/)), so the operating model is fixed:

- **Do NOT ship US orders as parcels from Toronto.** Per-parcel brokerage ($50–150) + duty makes a sub-$50 item unsellable into the US that way.
- **Bulk-import once** to the US as a **Non-Resident Importer** (broker sets up NRI + CBP bond 🚩), clear duty on the pallet, then **fulfill domestically** via 3PL + WFS.
- **Tax spine:** sole prop to start; **incorporate when you commit to US import** (liability rises) 🚩 lawyer. **GST/HST:** consider voluntary early registration to recover input tax credits on inventory/3PL (US sales are zero-rated exports) 🚩 CPA. **W-8BEN-E** to Walmart claiming treaty Art. VII (no US PE) 🚩 CPA. **A 3PL/WFS warehouse generally does not by itself create a US permanent establishment — but get this in writing** ([taxpage](https://taxpage.com/articles-and-tips/how-to-navigate-irs-form-w-8ben-e-for-canadian-businesses-with-american-suppliers/)). **Sales tax:** Walmart remits in ~40 states automatically; your exposure is Shopify direct sales crossing a state's economic-nexus threshold (usually $100k) — Shopify Tax monitors it.
- **Budget ~$1,500–4,000 CAD** for the initial CPA + customs broker engagement. Cheapest insurance you will buy.

## 5. Weekly KPI dashboard (one phone screen)

Review every Monday (SOP-6). Targets are starting reference points; refine with real data.

| KPI | What it tells you | Green | Watch | Red |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Units sold / wk (by channel)** | Demand & momentum | ↑ WoW | flat | ↓ 2+ wks |
| **Contribution margin %** | Are you making money per unit | ≥ 40% (Walmart) / ≥ 20% (DTC) | 10–20% | < 10% / negative |
| **Blended CAC** | Acquisition efficiency | < $25 | $25–40 | > 40 (kills sub-$50 margin) |
| **ROAS (paid, once on)** | Ad viability | ≥ 3× | 2–3× | < 2× → pause |
| **Walmart organic rank / share** | Free-traffic flywheel | rising | flat | falling |
| **Reviews count & avg ★** | Conversion & trust | ≥ 4.3★, growing | 4.0–4.3 | < 4.0 |
| **Weeks of inventory cover** | Stockout / overstock risk | 6–10 wks | 4–6 or 10–14 | < 4 or > 14 |
| **Content: posts/wk & top clip views** | Top-of-funnel health | cadence met, ≥1 breakout/mo | cadence met, no breakout | cadence missed |
| **Cash on hand & lowest projected point** | Survival | > 3 mo runway | 1.5–3 mo | < 1.5 mo |
| **Return rate** | Product quality signal | < 5% | 5–8% | > 8% (product problem) |

**Rule:** any **Red** is the week's priority. Two reds on margin/cash → freeze spend and reorders until resolved.

## 6. The phone-run weekly rhythm

- **Mon (45 min):** Weekly close + decisions (SOP-6).
- **Tue–Wed (batch):** Shoot + edit the week's content; queue in Buffer; DM new creators.
- **Thu:** Review creator posts, repost UGC, reply to comments/DMs, check ad performance.
- **Fri:** Supplier/3PL/broker touchpoints; reorder check; inbox zero on exceptions.
- **Daily (10–15 min):** Approve the day's auto-post, answer escalated support, watch the dashboard's cash + reviews tiles.
- **Outsourced/automated runs 24/7** underneath all of this.

This is a genuinely phone-runnable operation **once Phase 1 is stood up** — but note honestly (see Report 06) that Phase 0–1 setup (supplier, samples, broker/NRI, listings, first content) is **not** light phone work; it is several intense weeks at a laptop. The business becomes phone-run *after* it is built, not during the build.
