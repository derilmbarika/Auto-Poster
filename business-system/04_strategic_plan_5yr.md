# 5-Year Strategic Plan
### Validate → launch hero → scale → coordinated multi-SKU brand → bridge personal platform → optional exit
**Prepared 2026-06-26 · Design-led desk/workspace gear brand · Toronto founder**

> Phase gates are deliberate: **do not advance a phase until its gate is met.** Each gate exists to protect capital and to quarantine the first-timer risk that killed the prior business (over-committing before proof). Dollar figures cross-reference the Financial Model (Report 03) and Operating Playbook (Report 05).

---

## The shape of the plan
One design-dense hero (a modular desk-organizer system with integrated cable management) becomes a coordinated, single-material/single-palette desk *brand*. Growth is funded from cashflow and crowdfunding, not debt or equity. The founder's existing personal tech-content platform stays out until the product is proven, then becomes a Year-2+ accelerant — never a crutch.

---

## YEAR 1 — VALIDATE & LAUNCH THE HERO

### Phase 0 (Months 1–4): Validate before you spend
**Goal: prove demand with real money before risking the budget.**
- Finalize hero design + samples; **pass the physical acceptance gate** (Report 02 §5). If samples fail, stop — re-spec or kill.
- Source supplier (China v1, design for a Vietnam/India second source); get HTS classification from a customs broker 🚩.
- **Run a Kickstarter pre-launch** (the recommended on-ramp): build a landing page + email waitlist, shoot the campaign video, launch. **This pre-sells PO1 and validates demand without risking the $30–50k.**
- Register as a sole proprietorship; engage a cross-border CPA; consider voluntary GST/HST registration 🚩.
- Build the brand-only content account (NOT the personal platform): start posting desk content, seed the first creators.

**GATE 0 → 1:** Kickstarter funds (or, if skipping KS, a Shopify.ca soft-launch shows a sane conversion rate + first 10–15 reviews). **No traction → do not order bulk inventory.**

### Phase 1 (Months 4–9): Launch the hero on owned + marketplace channels
**Goal: a converting product on Shopify + Walmart.ca, profitable per unit.**
- Fulfill Kickstarter backers; roll the proven SKU onto **Shopify (brand home) + Walmart.ca**.
- Build listings (title, 6–8 images, A+ copy, cable-diameter range, magnet hold-force). Drive the review flywheel.
- Continue creator seeding (gift 20–40 micro-creators); pay the top 3–5 for UGC with usage rights.
- **US entry** once the page converts: customs broker → NRI → one small US bulk import → ShipMonk/boutique 3PL + WFS for Walmart.com. Incorporate around here (liability rises) 🚩. File W-8BEN-E 🚩.

**GATE 1 → 2:** 3 consecutive months contribution-positive **and** reorder driven by real sell-through. Then turn on paid ads (only on proven creative, ≥3× ROAS).

### Phase 1.5 (Months 9–12): First scale + Q4
- Q4 inventory buy (the year's biggest cash event — see Report 03 lowest-cash point). Ride BFCM + holiday gifting + the January "new desk setup" bump.
- Turn on paid (Meta/TikTok retargeting first, then prospecting only if ROAS ≥ 2–3×).

**Year-1 milestones (base):** ~5,850 units · ~$246k revenue · operating-profitable · 1 hero SKU live · lowest cash ~$22k · a review base, a content library, and a seeded creator network.

## YEAR 2 — PROVE THE SYSTEM IS A SYSTEM (multi-SKU begins)
**Goal: turn one hero into a coordinated 3-SKU line; bring in the personal platform.**
- Launch **SKU 2 and SKU 3** in the same material+palette (e.g., the standalone cable anchor as its own hero, then a monitor riser / pen-and-EDC module). Fund from cashflow + a second Kickstarter for the bigger SKU.
- **Bridge in the personal tech-content platform now** — the product is proven, reviews exist, the brand has a look. Use it as an organic accelerant (authentic "this is what I've been building" content, not a hard sell). This is the one moment it earns its keep.
- Hire the first outsourced help: a VA for support, a freelance media buyer for paid.
- Add a US warehouse footprint; deepen Walmart.com; test one more channel (e.g., a curated retailer or Faire wholesale).

**Milestones (base):** ~18,000 units · ~$828k revenue · 3 SKUs · founder takes first salary (~$40k) · paid ads contributing profitably.

**GATE 2 → 3:** the line sells as a *system* (attach rate / multi-item orders rising), not three unrelated SKUs; LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1.

---

## YEAR 3 — SCALE THE COORDINATED BRAND
**Goal: a recognizable 6-SKU desk label.**
- Expand to **~6 SKUs**, including the first **higher-AOV anchor** (a premium desk mat with a magnetic edge that docks the cable system — the piece that ties the whole range together; arrives now, not earlier, so it launches into an existing audience as a halo).
- Build a real brand moat: signature photography, a community, an email/SMS LTV engine, possibly a small "drop" cadence (collabs / limited colorways) à la Orbitkey.
- Consider a second-source factory (Vietnam/India) to cut tariff exposure and de-risk supply.

**Milestones (base):** ~40,000 units · ~$2.0M revenue · 6 SKUs · profitable, semi-automated, genuinely phone-run.

---

## YEAR 4 — BECOME A CATEGORY BRAND
**Goal: a brand people recognize on a desk-tour video.**
- **~10 SKUs**; full coordinated range (cable system, organizers, stands, mat, catch-alls) in 3–4 colorways.
- Expand distribution: more retail / wholesale (Faire, curated design shops), international (UK/EU/AU where Orbitkey/Ugmonk demand proves the audience).
- Brand becomes the moat: the named look, the review base, the content library, and the owned audience are now hard to copy.

**Milestones (base):** ~70,000 units · ~$3.8M revenue · 10 SKUs · a recognizable label.

---

## YEAR 5 — SCALE OR PREP THE EXIT
**Goal: a ~$6M, ~14-SKU brand that is either a durable cashflow machine or an acquisition target.**
- **~14 SKUs**; the coordinated design system is complete and recognizable.
- Decision point: **keep compounding** (it throws off ~$1M+ operating profit, phone-run) **or prep a sale** (see Report 07 for value drivers and what a buyer pays for).
- If selling: clean books, documented SOPs, transferable supplier relationships, an owned audience and email list, defensible reviews/ratings, and brand IP — the things that drive multiple.

**Milestones (base):** ~110,000 units · ~$6.4M revenue · ~$1.1M operating profit · optional exit ready.

---

## What could change the plan (decision triggers)
- **Conservative scenario:** stretch each phase by ~6–12 months; lean harder on Walmart search + organic (cheapest CAC); delay the personal-platform bridge until proof is unambiguous. Still a viable, slower path.
- **Downside scenario:** if Year 1 tracks to downside (Report 03) — **do not reinvest.** Hold position, recover the inventory, and decide between a pivot (different hero SKU) or an orderly wind-down. Kill criteria in Report 06.
- **Orbitkey Grid pressure:** if the modular-organizer niche gets crowded, lean **more cable-forward and material-forward** to occupy the white space they leave open, rather than competing head-on on trays.

> The throughline: **every phase is funded by the success of the previous one.** No phase bets money the business hasn't yet earned or pre-sold. That discipline is the antidote to the prior failure.
